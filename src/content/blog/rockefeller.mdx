John D. Rockefeller built Standard Oil into the world's largest company by 1880, controlling 90% of US oil refining. His business practices revolutionized American industry:

- Vertical integration
- Economies of scale
- Aggressive competitive tactics

Though controversial, Rockefeller's philanthropy established modern charitable foundations, donating over $500 million before his death.
